CTF Challenges

Here are the challenge categories we've got for this meet: Reverse Engineering+exploitation. Challenges have a guesstimate of how many points they'd be worth in an average CTF. It's OK to get stuck and have no idea how to do something. Flag down one of the people walking around helping, we're here to make sure you can solve these.

tips
  • The flag format varies, you'll know when you get one(or ask us)
  • Descriptions might contain hints
  • Harder challenges might contain spoilers for easier ones
  • Keep track of the flags/keys you get! We might(maybe) think of something fun to do with them. maybe.

Exploitation - 100

due to technical difficulties please try the medium difficulty exploitation or the very basic pwnables @ pwnable.kr(we've solved these too and can help you)

if you get further in picoctf there are exploitation challenges there as well!

things you'll need/want for pwnable.kr:

  • ncat (you can apt-get install nmap on debian to grab this, it comes with kali)
  • ida pro(check with orgs for tools file)
  • the assembly reference at the top of this page

I'm still new!

That's fine too! Register for picoctf here(don't need to give a valid email or anything): https://picoctf.com/registration

When you log in, you probably want to click "Switch to basic problem viewer" under their mini-game to see just the problems.

The key to solving them is thinking like a CTF player:

  • Do you see any phrases/words/numbers you might be able to google to try to get the answer? (try not to click on links that are writeups for the challenge! those don't exist for live CTFs)
  • Is the wording/phrasing/story of the challenge hinting at something?
  • Is there a tool I can find to do what I think I need to do? Google might know! (so will any of our helpers)
  • Go over what you know about the problem in your head, you might come up with an answer to one of the above.
